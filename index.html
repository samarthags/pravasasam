Parnakuteera (ಪರ್ಣ ಕುಟೀರ) is coastal Karnataka's best-kept secret — a village at the mouth of the Aghanaashini River surrounded by mangroves, forts and hidden waterfalls. Day 1 covers boat rides, Mirjan Fort, Vibhuthi Falls, Yana Rocks, water sports at Gokarna, hidden beaches, a night bonfire and party. Day 2 is Boreala Beach sunrise, Murudeshwar scuba diving, the Honnavara–Mavingondi route, Pilares Hanging Bridge and Jog Falls — then back to Bengaluru by night.

Beyond the obvious

Karnataka's hidden gems

Secret beaches and untouched spots most travel agencies don't even know about. We do.

Coastal Secret
Boreala Beach
A pristine, crowd-free beach near Bhatkal that locals guard jealously. White sands, fishing boats, and absolutely no commercial noise. Best at sunrise.
Tip: Visit before 7 AM for a completely deserted beach experience.
Hidden Waterfall
Vibhuthi Falls
A multi-tiered forest waterfall hidden near Parnakuteera that most tour operators skip entirely. Crystal clear pools, lush canopy and completely off the tourist map.
Tip: Best after monsoon — October to January for full flow.
Forest Fort
Mirjan Fort
A 16th-century coastal fort swallowed by forest and creepers near Gokarna. Incredibly photogenic ruins with watchtowers and moat — and virtually no crowds even in peak season.
Tip: Visit early morning for misty forest light through the ruins.
Rock Formation
Yana Rocks
Towering black crystalline limestone rock formations erupting from the Western Ghats forest — Bhairaveshwara and Mohini peaks. A UNESCO-worthy natural wonder most Bangaloreans have never seen.
Tip: Start the trek early — the forest trail stays cool until 10 AM.
River Bridge
Pilares Hanging Bridge
An iconic suspension bridge over the Sharavathi River between Honnavara and Mavingondi — crossing it with a valley view on both sides is a surreal experience that doesn't appear on any tourist map.
Tip: The bridge sways gently — hold the rails and look down for the thrill.
Backwater Estuary
Aghanaashini River Estuary
A pristine river estuary at Parnakuteera where the Aghanaashini River meets the sea. Boat through mangrove channels, watch kingfishers and spot otters at dawn. A photographer's dream.
Tip: Book the sunrise boat — start by 6 AM for golden light and wildlife.

FAQ

Common questions

Straight answers, no fluff.

What's the booking and cancellation policy?

50% advance to confirm. Cancellations: 7+ days before = 80% refund. 3–6 days = 50% refund. Last 48 hours = no refund. Adventure activities cancelled due to weather = that portion refunded.

Do I need experience for scuba diving?

No experience needed. Certified instructors give a full briefing before every dive. Age 10+ and basic swimming ability required. Medical conditions must be disclosed in advance.

What documents do I need to carry?

Every traveller must carry a valid government ID — Aadhaar Card, Driving License, or Passport. Mandatory for hotel check-ins and adventure activity registration.

Are meals included in the package?

Economy packages include breakfast only. Premium packages include all meals (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner). All food features authentic local coastal cuisine including seafood and Tulu Nadu dishes.

Is the trip suitable for mixed groups?

All packages welcome mixed groups, solo travellers, boys-only and girls-only groups. Safe, comfortable accommodation with proper room configurations for every group type.

What vehicle is used for transport?

AC Tempo Travellers (12–14 seats) and Innova/Ertiga for smaller groups. Pickup from Bengaluru Majestic, Yeshwanthpur, and Goraguntepalya. Overnight departure ~10 PM reaches coast by morning.

Where exactly is Parnakuteera and why is it special?

Parnakuteera (ಪರ್ಣ ಕುಟೀರ) is a small coastal village in Uttara Kannada district, situated at the mouth of the Aghanaashini River. It is surrounded by mangroves, fishing hamlets and forest, making it a perfect off-the-beaten-path base for exploring Gokarna, Mirjan Fort, Yana Rocks and Vibhuthi Falls.
